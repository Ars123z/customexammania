Complex Numbers Notes

Definition

An expression of the form x + iy, where x and y are real numbers, and i is a symbol is called a complex number and is usually denoted by z.

x is called the real part, and y is called the imaginary part of z.

Equality of Complex Numbers

Definition

Two complex numbers $x_{1} + iy_{1}$ and $x_{2} + iy_{2}$ are equal if and only if $x_{1} = x_{2}$ and $y_{1} = y_{2}$

Algebra of Complex Numbers

Addition: The sum of two complex numbers, $x_{1} + iy_{1}$ and $x_{2} + iy_{2}$ is the complex number $x_{1} + x_{2} + i(y_{1} + y_{2})$

SubtractionThe diffrence of two complex numbers, $x_{1} + iy_{1}$ and $x_{2} + iy_{2}$ is the complex number $x_{1} - x_{2} + i(y_{1} - y_{2})$

MultiplicationThe diffrence of two complex numbers, $x_{1} + iy_{1}$ and $x_{2} + iy_{2}$ is the complex number $x_{1}x_{2} - y_{1}y_{2} + i(x_{1}y_{2} - x_{2}y_{1})$
